  • An end to tag soup with XHTML

  • Time-based presentations with SMIL
  • Animated, scalable graphics with SVG
  • The lingua franca of Science - mathematics and MathML

The Mobile Web Initiative

Has the goal to make Web access from a mobile device as simple, easy, and convenient as Web access from a desktop device.

Current focus

  • developing "best practices"
  • "mobileOK" trustmark for Web sites
    • machine readable and aimed at search engines
  • work on device information needed for content adaptation
  • marketing and outreach activities

Established and maintained through the financial support of sponsors

Rich Web Clients Activity

  • Compound Document Formats
    • Specifying behavior for combinations such as XHTML, SVG, SMIL and XForms
  • Web APIs
    • Focusing on standardizing established practice for web page scripting APIs such as XMLHttpRequest (AJAX), setTimeout, and the Window object
  • Web Application Formats
    • Adopt, refine and possibly extend existing practices where possible
    • Binding language such as XBL, and declarative user interface language, such as XUL

Intelligent Content Adaptation

  • Protocols and APIs for device capabilities and user preferences
    • Composite Capability/Preference Profiles (CC/PP)
    • Delivery Context: Interfaces (DCI)
    • Content Selection for Device Independence (DISelect)
    • CSS Media queries and @media handheld
    • Use of XSLT and other transformational techniques
  • Authoring languages for content adaptation
    • Designed for machine transformation to meet the needs of specific devices and user preferences
    • Emphasis on declarative representations
    • Can take advantage of latest techniques as you don't need to wait for client-side support
      • for example for XHTML 2 and XForms

The promise of multimodality

  • Giving users the means to interact through speech, pen, visual displays and tactile interfaces

IETF Widex Working Group

Developing a protocol for remote user interfaces based upon the Model-View-Controller paradigm, where the UI is expressed in terms of an XML DOM and the protocol is independent of the markup language.

Ubiquitous Computing

Ubiquitous computing postulates a world where people are surrounded by computing devices and a computing infrastructure that supports us in everything we do.

The Ubiquitous Web

  • Synthesis between the World Wide Web and Ubiquitous Computing
    • Applicable to home networks, offices, factory floor, mobile, cars, etc.
  • Making it much easier to build distributed applications across a diversity of networked devices
    • Enabling applications to be created using markup, style sheets and scripts
  • Context awareness - dynamically adapting to users, devices and environmental conditions
    • Exploiting the features of each device, and not dropping to the lowest common denominator
    • Giving users the means to select the modes of interaction that best meet their needs, subject to the capabilities of the devices
    • Extending device capabilities through access to network services and through combination with other devices
    • Providing remote user interfaces to devices and services
      • W3C/IETF collaboration on protocol for passing DOM events

The Ubiquitous Web

  • Application mobility - enabling users to seamlessly switch between devices whilst continuing with the same application
    • seamless in the sense of not requiring significant conscious effort from users
  • A greater focus on the network rather than on particular devices, and an associated need to address trust, security, privacy and identity
    • Combining local information with network-based information sources
    • Your data lives in the network, but applications need to allow for temporary lapses in connectivity
  • Providing the Web developer with a clean model of distributed applications
    • URI's for describing devices, services and sessions
    • A means to pass DOM events and updates between devices
    • A Web-based approach to resource discovery and binding within temporary and persistent sessions

Example: smart meeting

You are scheduled to meet with colleagues and plan to show a presentation using your cellphone as a controller and a networked wall screen as the display device.

  • Your slides are on the Web and linked from the agenda
  • The corporate web server knows such things as:
    • Who is expected to attend the meeting and the meeting agenda
    • A floor plan for your building and what resources are available in each meeting room
    • Details of your mobile phone and other personal devices
  • When you arrive in the meeting room
    • The Bluetooth interface on the wall screen senses whose phones and notebooks are in the general neighborhood
    • The meeting application running on the corporate server compares this to who is expected to attend
    • Your phone is automatically set up to allow you to present your slides
    • The audio and slide transitions are captured by the network for people who want to review it at a later date

Trust, Security, Privacy and Identity

  • Increasingly sensitive information and services will reside in the network
    • You will be willing to share information with people you trust
    • This is necessary to fulfil the potential of the Web for rich applications
  • Trust is bounded and intransitive
    • You trust your bank with your personal finances, but not with your private life
    • You trust Mary, and Mary trusts John, but this doesn't mean you trust John
  • Information access can be modelled in terms of identity and group membership
    • Membership of a group entitles access to information available to that group
    • Proof of membership is dependent on proving that you have a given identity (or persona)
    • Organizations can also be treated as persona
  • Digital Rights Descriptions and Digital Rights Management

Trust and devices

  • You are in an airport lounge or a hotel room and wish to print a company confidential document
  • Where is the printer, and can it be trusted not to save a copy or to forward a copy onto someone else?
  • One idea is to walk up to the printer and give it your document via a USB thumb drive, or via bluetooth from your phone
  • You may also be able to ask the printer to prove its identity and to compare that to the one shown on the airport/hotel's website
    • A trusted physical communication path between the printer and your mobile device
    • Based upon public keys and security certificates signed by a trusted agency

W3C Workshop on Transparency and Usability of Web Authentication, 15-16 March, New York

Access to network based capabilities

Examples:

  • Geographic location service
    • Device includes GPS front end and passes this data to a server where 3rd party service converts this into the location format needed by the application
    • This is passed back to the device and exposed as a DOM Event
    • The web application doesn't need to know the details and instead is only concerned about the location format and frequency of updates
  • Speech services
    • An audio extension streams audio to/from the network
    • This is exposed as a JavaScript object that supports speech synthesis and recognition through access to remote speech services
    • The Web application doesn't need to know the details of the protocol used to access the remote speech services

Layered approach to device coordination

  • Descriptions of devices and services
    • URI's for describing devices, services and sessions
    • This enables the use of RDF and the Semantic Web
    • That makes it easy to combine distributed information sources
    • Also a role for more informal collective approaches (folksonomies)
  • Discovery and binding
    • Relies on access to multiple information sources
    • To find a nearby printer, first find out where you are, then find printers in nearby locations
    • Web applications don't need to know details of discovery protocols
    • Discovery can be supported through Web servers and implemented in terms of existing Web Services standards such as SOAP, WSDL, UDDI, or simpler use of HTTP (REST)
    • Restricted role for UPnP (universal plug and play)

Discovery and Home Networks

  • Users of home networks require easy configuration and diagnosis of problems when faults occur
  • You purchase a new wireless printer and power it up
  • How does the printer know which network to attach to?
    • Your neighbor's network may have a stronger signal
  • Solution involves telling your printer about your network, or your network about the printer, or both
  • This involves the user in some way, but how can we simplify this?
    • One idea is to use RFID tags
    • Another is to use your mobile phone as a reader for 2D bar codes
  • Discovery may involve some human intervention!

Extending the Web into the Physical World

  • Most people associate the Web with browsing websites from a desktop computer, or perhaps a smart phone
  • Web technologies can also be applied to applications involving devices acting as sensors and effectors
  • These can be used throughout the home, office, or factory, etc.
  • Applications are written with a combination of markup and scripting with events that are passed between devices
  • Layered architecture hides details of what communication protocols are used at the transport layer
  • Web application has a simple view of the sensor/effector as a DOM object that you can set properties and event handlers

W3C Ubiquitous Web Workshop

Keio University, Tokyo, 9-10 March 2006

  • To explore the vision of the Web as a distributed applications platform that works across a wide range of devices in areas such as offices, home networks, mobile, automotive, aviation, etc. with the potential for increasing the range and reducing the cost of developing and deploying such applications.
  • To explain how current W3C work fits into this vision, e.g. work on Web Application API's, Delivery Context Interfaces, Device Descriptions, Multimodal Architecture, etc.
  • To identify and prioritize additional areas which would benefit from standardization, in particular, the integration of sessions and device coordination into web applications.
